The Word “Captivate”

A Vocabulary and Meaning

Etymology

From Latin Captivus, past participate of captivare meaning "to take or seize," derived from captivus meaning "prisoner, captive."

Definition

A captivate is a word used to attract and hold the attention or interest of someone strongly.

Denotation

In its literal sense, captivate refers to:

  • Holding someone's attention Fascating
  • Strongly interesting someone
Example (Denotation):
The magician captivated the audience with his tricks.

Connotation

Beyond its literal meaning, captive often suggests:

  • Charm or allure
  • Emotional or mental fascination
Example (Connotation):
Her voice captivated him, leaving him completely enchanted.
